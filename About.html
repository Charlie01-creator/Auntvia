The Aunt Via Story

Aunt Via Cakes began in 2010 not in a commercial bakery, but in the warm, flour-dusted kitchen of Virginia "Aunt Via" Anderson's cozy home. What started as a passionate hobby—baking birthday cakes for family and friends—quickly grew into something much more special. Word spread through the neighborhood, then the city, about the magical cakes that tasted like childhood memories and looked like works of art.

Via's secret wasn't just in her family recipes (passed down through three generations), but in her philosophy: every cake should tell a story. She believed that celebrations aren't just dates on a calendar—they're milestones, memories in the making, moments of joy that deserve to be honored with something extraordinary.

"A cake isn't just dessert. It's the centerpiece of celebration, the sweet punctuation at the end of a beautiful sentence, the edible memory that lingers long after the last crumb is gone."

- Virginia "Aunt Via" Anderson, Founder

By 2015, what began as a home kitchen operation had grown into a dedicated bakery with a team of passionate bakers. But despite the growth, Aunt Via's original vision remains at our core: to create cakes that engage all the senses, that look like art, smell like heaven, and taste even better than they look.

Today, we serve thousands of customers across the city, creating cakes for birthdays, weddings, anniversaries, and every celebration in between. But whether it's a simple birthday cake or an elaborate five-tier wedding masterpiece, each creation receives the same attention to detail, the same commitment to quality, and the same generous sprinkle of love that Via baked into her very first cake.

Our Journey

2010

The Beginning

Aunt Via starts baking cakes for family and friends in her home kitchen. Her first paid order is a birthday cake for a neighbor's child.

2012
First Wedding Cake

Via creates her first wedding cake for a family wedding. Word begins to spread about her exceptional talent.

2015

First Bakery

With growing demand, Via opens her first small bakery on Baker Street with two employees.

2018

Expansion

The bakery expands to include a dedicated wedding cake studio and hires its first pastry chef trained in Europe.

2020

Pandemic Adaptation

Despite challenges, we adapt with contactless delivery and safety protocols, becoming a trusted source of celebration during difficult times.

2023

Today

We now serve thousands of customers annually with a team of 12 dedicated professionals, while maintaining the personal touch that started it all.
